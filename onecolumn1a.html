Estimating The Ages Of Galaxies

In this project I led a 4 person team on a large CCD telescope designed to image galaxies. The top left picture (below) shows the carefully planned observation plan for the telescope, planned using using predictive astronomy software (Stellarium). The top right picture shows the galaxy image that I took of the M52 galaxy. In the bottom left you can see how a technique called 'photometry' gives us flux (light density) values, that we can then use to represent the relative brightness (bottom right) of different galaxies against standard light sources to guess where these galaxies are in there evolution.

Planning A Space Mission

My 6 person team recieved a distinction for replanning a space mission that had operational failures to land and sample the surface of a comet. There are extreme limitations on the weight and electric power you can take with you into space, and we successfully navigated some very difficult trade offs between mission objectives and technical limitations. The picture in the top left (below) shows part of the refined specifications that were finally agreed upon. Pictured in the top right was the key piece of equipment needed to carry out the sampling and analysis. In the bottom left you can see a computer generated image of the actual spacecraft called a 'lander' that would make the long journey via several gravitational slingshots to reach the comet. In the bottom right you can see a grapical reproduction from Wikipedia of the comet - 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko - that we were aiming at. It was a lack of detail about the comets surface that caused the previous lander to fall sideways upon contact with the comet surface.
