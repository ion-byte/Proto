My first degree was in manufacturing of sports equipment for which I recieved a 2.2(Hons) BSc from Loughborough University. In this degree I learned many different manufacturing techniques such as investment casting and rapid prototyping. Computer Aided Design (CAD) is used to visualise and stress test new designs. Here are some pictures of a prototype design I completed in my spare time 2011 using CAD software of a new type of mobile dumbbell rack (grey bucket trolley!). I was seeing people getting back injuries in the gyms, not due to the exercises themselves but due to the set up to the exercises and I tasked myself to see if I could design a solution. This rack would keep people safe during the set up phase of a chest press, by allowing the user to rack the dumbbells (shown in red!) on the trolley and them take them off at full arm extension.
